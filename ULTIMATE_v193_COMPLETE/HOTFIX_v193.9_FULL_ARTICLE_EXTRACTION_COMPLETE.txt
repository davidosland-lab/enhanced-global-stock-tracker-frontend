PROBLEM SUMMARY
================================================================================

After v193.8 fixed the article truncation bug (5 → 11 articles), the World 
Event Risk monitor correctly detected "nuclear_threat" topics with a base 
risk of 100/100. However:

❌ Fear Index: 0.00 (expected 0.40–0.70)
❌ Anger Index: 0.00 (expected 0.30–0.60)
❌ Emotion Boost: 0.0 points (expected 10–20 points)
❌ Sentiment Boost: 0.0 points (expected 2–5 points)

ROOT CAUSE:
The World Event Risk monitor was analyzing only article TITLES (typically 
10–15 words), not the full article text. For geopolitical crisis articles 
about Iran–Israel conflicts, nuclear threats, military escalation:
  - Titles: "Oil prices surge on Middle East tensions"
  - Full text contains: "fears of regional escalation", "anger over 
    retaliation", "panic in oil markets", "threat of wider conflict"

Emotion keywords (fear, panic, anger, retaliation) appear ~50x more 
frequently in full article text than in titles alone.
