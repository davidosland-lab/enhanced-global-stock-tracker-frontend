IS THIS CORRECT OR A BUG?
================================================================================

TWO POSSIBLE INTERPRETATIONS:
------------------------------

INTERPRETATION 1: Current Design is CORRECT
--------------------------------------------
Philosophy: "Gap prediction should be objective (market data only)"

Rationale:
- Gap prediction uses hard data (futures, prices, indices)
- Article sentiment is subjective (text analysis)
- Mixing subjective sentiment with objective prices reduces prediction accuracy
- Sentiment affects OVERALL score, but not gap calculation
- This separates concerns: gap = market mechanics, sentiment = mood

Flow:
  Market Data → Gap Prediction (objective)
  Articles → Sentiment (subjective)
  Both → Combined in overall sentiment score

INTERPRETATION 2: Current Design is INCOMPLETE
-----------------------------------------------
Philosophy: "Gap prediction should incorporate all available signals"

Rationale:
- Articles contain forward-looking information (Fed policy, earnings, etc.)
- Strong negative news can override bullish futures
- Example: Futures up +0.5% but Fed announces rate hike → gap might be negative
- Article sentiment adds context to raw price movements
- Your original request said: "gap prediction should incorporate sentiment from the whole article"

Proposed Flow:
  Market Data + Article Sentiment → Gap Prediction (comprehensive)
  Gap Prediction → Adjusts sentiment score
