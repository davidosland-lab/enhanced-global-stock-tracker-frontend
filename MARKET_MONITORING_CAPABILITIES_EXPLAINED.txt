Question: Does the system monitor AORD, FTSE, S&P, NASDAQ movement while
          markets are open to identify direction and momentum?

SHORT ANSWER: NO - The system uses SCHEDULED overnight analysis, NOT continuous
real-time monitoring during market hours.

================================================================================
HOW THE SYSTEM ACTUALLY WORKS
================================================================================

EXECUTION MODEL: Scheduled Batch Processing (Overnight Pipelines)
------------------------------------------------------------------

The system runs THREE separate overnight pipelines:

1. AU OVERNIGHT PIPELINE (runs BEFORE ASX opens at 10:00 AM AEST)
   - Analyzes US market CLOSES from previous day
   - Analyzes UK market CLOSE
   - Predicts ASX opening gap
   - Scans 240 ASX stocks
   - Generates trade signals ONCE per day

2. UK OVERNIGHT PIPELINE (runs BEFORE LSE opens at 8:00 AM GMT)
   - Analyzes US market CLOSES from previous day
   - Analyzes Asian market CLOSES
   - Predicts FTSE opening gap
   - Scans 240 UK stocks
   - Generates trade signals ONCE per day

3. US OVERNIGHT PIPELINE (runs BEFORE NYSE opens at 9:30 AM EST)
   - Analyzes Asian market CLOSES
   - Analyzes European market CLOSES
   - Predicts S&P/NASDAQ opening direction
   - Scans 480 US stocks
   - Generates trade signals ONCE per day

TIMING:
- Each pipeline runs ONCE per night
- Uses CLOSED market data (not intraday data)
- Predicts OPENING gap/direction
- Trade signals generated BEFORE market open
- NO continuous monitoring during trading hours

ACTUAL MONITORING FLOW
================================================================================

OVERNIGHT PIPELINE EXECUTION (e.g., AU Pipeline):
--------------------------------------------------

Step 1: SCHEDULED START (e.g., 8:00 AM AEST, before market open)
  python scripts/run_au_pipeline_v1.3.13.py --mode full

Step 2: FETCH CLOSED MARKET DATA (one-time snapshot)
  - Get US market CLOSE from previous day (^GSPC, ^IXIC, ^DJI)
  - Calculate: S&P +0.8%, NASDAQ +1.4% (yesterday's close-to-close)
  - Get commodity closes: AUD/USD +0.9%, Iron Ore +0.0%, Oil -6.4%
  - Get VIX close: 18.5 (fear gauge)

Step 3: PREDICT OPENING GAP (one-time calculation)
  - US markets up -> predict ASX opens +0.5% to +1.0%
  - Confidence: 75% (based on historical correlation)
  - Direction: BULLISH

Step 4: SCAN 240 STOCKS (one-time scan)
  - Fetch overnight data for each stock
  - Calculate technical indicators
  - Run ML predictions
  - Score opportunities (with new 45 threshold)

Step 5: GENERATE TRADE SIGNALS (one-time output)
  - Top 10-20 opportunities identified
  - 5-12 BUY/SELL signals generated
  - Signals sent to paper trading system

Step 6: PIPELINE COMPLETES (15-25 minutes total)
  - Report saved: reports/screening/au_morning_report.json
  - Pipeline exits
  - NO continuous monitoring starts

================================================================================
WHY THIS DESIGN?
================================================================================

RATIONALE FOR OVERNIGHT-ONLY ANALYSIS:
---------------------------------------

1. COMPUTATIONAL EFFICIENCY
   - Running 240-480 stock analysis + ML predictions takes 15-25 minutes
   - Doing this every 5 minutes would be impossible
   - Daily close data is more stable than intraday noise

2. DATA COSTS & RATE LIMITS
   - Real-time intraday data requires expensive subscriptions
   - Yahoo Finance (free tier) has rate limits
   - Daily close data is free and reliable

3. TRADING STRATEGY ALIGNMENT
   - System designed for OVERNIGHT gap trading
   - Predicts OPENING direction based on overnight news/sentiment
   - Executes trades at MARKET OPEN
   - Not designed for intraday momentum trading

4. TECHNICAL ANALYSIS STABILITY
   - Daily candles more reliable than 5-minute candles
   - ML models trained on daily data
   - Technical indicators (RSI, MA) calculated on daily timeframe

5. NEWS SENTIMENT INTEGRATION
   - FinBERT analyzes overnight news
   - News impact assessed once per day
   - Real-time news sentiment would be extremely noisy
